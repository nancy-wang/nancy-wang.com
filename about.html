Welcome to my corner of the Internet! I'm Nancy. I’m a product manager, community builder and action-oriented optimist passionate about harnessing today’s tools to create a better future—and finding awesome people to do it with me! Currently, I lead the development of new task management experiences for Microsoft Planner and serve on the advisory committee for Breaktime, a nonprofit focused on breaking the cycle of young adult homelessness.

How did I get here?

Through stargazing trips and hiking trails, my family taught me that our Earth is singlehandedly one of the most beautiful and priceless gifts bestowed to us. From an early age, the word "sustainability" took over my life and career, from small things like persuading my parents to use tote bags and buy water filters, to leading a school-wide campaign to compost food scraps and publishing a mobile app that told people how to dispose of their trash sustainably. Through these projects, I began to view technology as an agent for creating social change. I decided to study Computer Science in college to learn how to build and use technology to effect positive changes in the world.

Living in Baltimore during my college years meant the city was just as much an educator as my professors: it exposed me to the interconnectedness of social problems and showed me how a united community is necessary for overcoming these issues. Through volunteering at the Johns Hopkins Hospital, I got the opportunity to assist low-income individuals with accessing resources like food stamps, healthcare, and employment. I realized then that I wanted to serve as an advocate for people who didn't have a seat at the table, and it was this desire that propelled me into my current job as a product manager.

What's next?

I'm currently exploring:

  • public speaking: I caught the public speaking bug after attempting a 5 minute talk on behavioral economics in a crowd of 500 people! It was exhilarating, and I'm now seeking other areas where I can share my knowledge in fun ways. Most recently, I gave a talk on making decisions as a product manager for Product Festival
  • chaotic acts of kindness: Something I've come to realize is that any time we deviate from the norm, we unleash an alternative reality of how the world could operate. Through my actions, I want to see how bringing kindness to unexpected places can shape the communities around us. These are already strange times, why not bend it closer to the reality ​we want to live in?
